What is Peripheral Neuropathy?

Peripheral neuropathy refers to damage affecting the peripheral nerves – those outside your brain and spinal cord. These nerves carry information between your central nervous system and the rest of your body. Understanding this condition is essential for informed discussions with healthcare professionals.

📈

Prevalence Data

Peripheral neuropathy affects approximately 25-30% of adults over 60. For individuals with diabetes, the prevalence rates may reach 60%, highlighting the importance of education about this condition.

⚡

Common Symptoms

Numbness that gradually spreads, tingling sensations, burning or freezing pain, heightened sensitivity, muscle weakness, and problems with coordination or balance.

🔍

Risk Factors

Various factors are associated with peripheral nerve issues including metabolic disorders, vitamin imbalances, environmental toxins, traumatic injuries, viral infections, and hereditary factors.

Common Symptoms

⚡

Numbness

A tingling or numb sensation that gradually spreads from the tips of your fingers and toes to your hands and feet.

🔍

Tingling

A prickling or itching sensation that can be mild or severe.

🔥

Burning

A burning sensation that can be mild or severe.

❄️

Freezing

A feeling of cold that can be mild or severe.

🤕

Pain

A sharp, burning, or aching pain that can be mild or severe.

🏃

Muscle Weakness

Weakness in the hands, feet, or other parts of the body.

🤸

Coordination Problems

Difficulty with fine motor skills or balance.
